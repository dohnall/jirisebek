Q3: Did the initiative/project support to EXISTING Innovation STRATEGIES?

Addressing the problem of effective knowledge management (e.g. impact of climate change on cities, consequences of ageing population for workforce skills, etc.). Addressing the problem of interactions between various GCs (e.g. impact of climate change on water and food security, impacts of ageing on migration). Addressing the problem of information overload, with studies at various levels of granularity, and considerable controversy in many topics. Addressing the problem that GCs are NOT just “big problems”. They represent agendas for RTD, innovation and the development of conducive environments for adoption of innovations. Addressing the problem of classifying GCs in terms of: (a) Geographical relevance; (b) Knowledge domain and RTD relevance; (c) Feasibility as an economic or social investment
Promoting interdisciplinary knowledge development Promoting multi-stakeholder contributions to and applications of this knowledge base Promoting a policy mix of actions (developing a policy roadmap that spans several traditionally distinct policy domains).
Applying horizon scanning & issue mapping Mapping issues against GCs and GRs Assessing factors shaping the trajectories of GCs & GRs Paying particular attention to (a) Seeds of change (aka “weak signals”) and (b) Potential surprises (aka “wild cards”) Promoting bottom-up approaches (e.g. surveys, citizen panels and web-based crowdsourcing) for the analysis of emerging issues relevant to GCs & GRs)
Overcoming language barriers, cultural differences, competition and fragmentation of knowledge across disciplines, professions, and localities. Exploiting creative environments, commercialisation, standardisation and innovations (technological & social) Improving access/location of codified knowledge Promoting validation/evaluation of knowledge Assessing implications of existing knowledge for action (both present and future) Locating and interconnecting knowledge communities Enabling better communication and networking between experts and stakeholders of different types
Identifying key features of different worldviews Enabling direct comparison of 'worldviews' around specific points Identifying key limitations of 'worldviews' abilities to grasp major problems and solutions Identifying key points of potential agreements about gaps in knowledge (though not necessarily about how to most effectively resolve these). Identifying key “boundary objects” for potential agreement about key features of a phenomenon, and alignment in terms of action without necessarily achieving consensus about many other things
Improving its performance in frontier research, Stimulating future and emerging technologies, Encouraging cross-border training and career development, and Supporting research infrastructures
Stimulating leadership in enabling and industrial technologies, Improving access to risk finance, and Stimulating innovation in SMEs
Making Europe more robust against the crisis (i.e. resilience) Reforming national research and innovation systems More and better EU funding for research and innovation Working in partnership to address societal challenges Maximising social and territorial cohesion
Getting top talent for Europe (i.e. education & training systems) Delivering the European Research Area (e.g. EU Funding Instruments) European Institute of Innovation and Technology
Access to finance for innovation companies Protecting & enhancing the value of intellectual property & boosting creativity Accelerating and modernising standard-setting Putting the power of the public purse (i.e. public procurement) to innovation
Pooling forces to achieve breakthroughs: European Innovation Partnerships (e.g. India, China and USA)
